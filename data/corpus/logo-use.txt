Obtaining permission to use FC Barcelona’s trademarked images or logos requires a formal process to ensure legal compliance. The first step is to identify exactly which logos, images, or branding elements you wish to use, and for what purpose. The intended use could be commercial, educational, media, or promotional, and clearly defining this helps the club determine whether permission can be granted and under what conditions. It is important to note that all uses must comply with the club’s intellectual property and brand guidelines.

Next, you should contact FC Barcelona’s licensing or legal department. This is typically done through the official club website, where you can find a contact form or email address specifically for licensing inquiries. Your request should include a detailed description of the materials you wish to use, the context and medium of use, the duration of use, and any distribution plans. Including information about your organization, project, or platform can help the club evaluate the request more efficiently.

Once the request is submitted, the club will review it and may ask for additional information or clarifications. If approved, they will issue a formal license or written permission that specifies the terms, such as usage limits, mandatory attribution, and any fees if applicable. You must adhere strictly to these terms to avoid infringement. After receiving authorization, you can legally use the images or logos according to the approved guidelines, and you may be required to report on or submit materials showing how the branding is used, depending on the agreement.
