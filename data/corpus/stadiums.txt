Since the start of the 2024–2025 season, FC Barcelona has used three different stadiums as their home grounds. This unusual arrangement was largely due to the ongoing renovations at the Camp Nou, which required the club to temporarily relocate some of their matches to alternative venues. Using multiple stadiums allowed Barcelona to continue hosting home games while the iconic stadium was being modernized and expanded.

The first alternative was the Estadi Olímpic Lluís Companys, which hosted several matches when Camp Nou was unavailable. This historic stadium, originally built for the 1992 Olympics, provided a larger capacity than the secondary option and allowed Barcelona to maintain a professional environment for both players and fans. At the same time, the club also utilized the Estadi Johan Cruyff, a smaller 6,000-seat venue primarily used for early-season matches and fixtures with lower attendance expectations. This stadium served as a practical solution for maintaining regular match scheduling while ensuring compliance with local permits and safety regulations.

Finally, in November 2025, Barcelona officially returned to the renovated Spotify Camp Nou, marking the reopening of their primary home stadium. This return was celebrated as a major milestone, allowing the team and supporters to experience the new facilities, increased capacity, and modernized amenities. The use of three stadiums over the course of two seasons underscores the club’s flexibility and careful planning to balance competitive needs with the logistical challenges of a major stadium renovation.
