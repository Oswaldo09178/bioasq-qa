Note that Barcelona training is closed by default, and only accredited media or approved analysts are allowed in.

Accessing Barcelona’s training sessions begins with confirming whether the specific session is open to external media. Most first-team trainings are closed, with only a few minutes opened for cameras during designated media days. As a blogger or analyst, you must first verify the schedule through FC Barcelona’s official website or the club’s media department announcements. Once you confirm that access is possible, prepare your credentials. This includes having an active blog, portfolio, or analytics platform that shows consistent football‑related work, as the club only considers requests with a clear professional purpose and established audience.

The next step is to submit a formal accreditation request to FC Barcelona’s Media Department. You do this through the club’s official media contact form or by emailing the communications office with full details. Your request must include your name, outlet or blog name, website links, audience metrics, the reason for attending the training session, and any equipment you plan to use. After the request is reviewed, the club will respond with approval or rejection. If approved, you will receive instructions about arrival times, security checks, permitted areas, and any restrictions on filming or photography. On the day of the session, you must present identification, follow staff guidance, and comply with all media rules to maintain access for future events.
