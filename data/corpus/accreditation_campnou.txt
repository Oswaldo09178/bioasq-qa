Requesting media accreditation to cover a FC Barcelona match involves several important steps that all journalists and media professionals must follow. The process begins with registering your media outlet with the club’s official accreditation system. This registration must be completed using a verified organizational email address and not a personal or generic email account. Once the registration is complete, you can access the accreditation request form for the specific match you want to cover. On this form, you will need to provide detailed information about your media outlet, your role such as whether you are a writer, photographer, or online journalist, and the details of the match including date and competition. Accreditation requests must be submitted within the time window established by the club, which is usually during the week of the match.

After submitting the request form, the club reviews the application to ensure it meets all requirements. Approval is not guaranteed and depends on factors such as available space, the nature of the media outlet, and compliance with the club’s media policies. Once your accreditation is approved, you will receive a confirmation, typically one to three days before the match. The confirmation includes instructions for collecting your media pass and details about the areas you will have access to inside the stadium.

On the day of the match, accredited media personnel must go to the designated accreditation center to collect their pass, usually a couple of hours before kickoff. You are required to bring a valid identification document along with your confirmation details. Once you have your media pass, you are allowed to access specific areas reserved for the press, such as the press box, mixed zone, and designated photography positions. Following these steps ensures that you can cover the match safely, efficiently, and in compliance with all stadium regulations while gaining access to key areas for reporting and photography.
