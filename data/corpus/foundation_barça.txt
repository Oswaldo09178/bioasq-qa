Collaborating with FC Barcelona on community or social projects starts with identifying the type of initiative you want to be involved in. The club runs numerous programs through its Barça Foundation, including youth education, sports development, social inclusion, and global outreach projects. Before reaching out, it is important to clearly define your objectives, the target audience, and the resources or expertise your organization can contribute. Understanding how your project aligns with the club’s social mission increases the likelihood of a successful collaboration.

The next step is to contact the club through the official Barça Foundation channels. You can do this by filling out a contact or collaboration form on the foundation’s website or sending a detailed proposal via email. Your proposal should include a description of the project, the goals, the expected impact, the timeline, and the roles of each party involved. Providing background information about your organization, previous projects, and potential partners also helps the foundation assess your credibility and capacity for collaboration.

Once your proposal is submitted, the Barça Foundation reviews it and may request additional information or a meeting to discuss the project in more detail. If the proposal is approved, the club works with you to finalize an agreement that outlines responsibilities, resources, timelines, and reporting requirements. During the project, regular communication ensures that objectives are met and that both parties can address challenges effectively. Upon completion, a review or evaluation may be conducted to measure impact and identify opportunities for future collaboration.
